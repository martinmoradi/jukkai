# Phase 1 research summary

Produced July 2, 2026. Reconciles `docs/research/audience.md`, `docs/research/competitors.md`, and
`docs/research/keywords.md` against frozen `docs/strategy/foundation.md` v1.1, then records the accepted v1.2
foundation amendments.

`docs/strategy/foundation.md` remains canonical. Phase 1 did not silently change it: Martin accepted
two amendments after review, and two items remain conditional/later.

## Strongest confirmed findings

- The audience priority order stands: private architecture clients, then B2B, then
  art visitors, then artists.
- The structural website job stands: qualified interior-architecture enquiries. The
  searchable, priced, high-intent demand is architecture, pricing, and B2B, not art
  buying.
- One canonical Rennes architecture service page at launch is confirmed by the live
  SERPs. Commune networks and department pages are the wrong shape.
- Price and fee opacity is the shared enemy across audiences. Public architecture
  pricing is both a trust move and a competitive wedge.
- The market leaves an empty position for Jukkai: a designed emotional world, public
  architecture pricing, and real proof in one place.
- The proof layer is unusually valuable. UNAID, 30 ans de métier, 2 architectes,
  carried-over Google reviews, and selected projects can beat a field where many
  competitors show either proof or pricing, rarely both.
- Châteaugiron is a local-discovery opportunity, but mostly through GBP, citations,
  physical-anchor copy, and later proof-backed local content.

## Open risks

- SERPs were refreshed live but not from a geolocated google.fr session. Local-pack
  and SERP-feature claims should be spot-checked before the Phase 4 SEO spec freezes.
- Art-buyer evidence is mostly seller and institution voice. The foundation's
  deferral to real foot traffic remains correct.
- B2B pre-purchase voice is scarce. The B2B page needs Crystelle's nameable
  references to become persuasive.
- Several Phase 0 questions remain load-bearing: launch portfolio, B2B references,
  entry-offer name/scope/price, testimonials, opening date, shop visuals, signage,
  Google review push, contact-flow preference, and artist dépôt/commission terms.
- IDKREA may return online, and national networks may escalate local pages. Recheck
  before IA freeze and again near launch.
- Art price transparency is a real tension: visible prices reduce intimidation, but
  pre-opening inventory is not stable enough to print numbers.

## Implications for messaging

- Lead structurally with architecture, but let the Jukkai world set the emotional
  register.
- Treat "écoute" as a proof theme. Show it through testimonials, process, and case
  studies, not as a loose claim.
- Put pricing, budget respect, and process clarity close to conversion. Mention the
  contrat or lettre de mission if made canonical.
- Place UNAID and the 30-year claim near conversion points, but let references,
  projects, and transformation stories do most of the persuasion.
- For art visitors, use coup de coeur, pousser la porte, and permission for
  neophytes. Avoid galerie, boutique, magasin, objets, and art-world jargon.
- For B2B, keep Jukkai's sensibility present but secondary to references, process,
  budget, deadlines, and coordination.

## Implications for sitemap

- Launch structure should protect three keyword-bearing commercial surfaces:
  `/architecte-interieur-rennes/`, the cost guide
  `/combien-coute-architecte-interieur-rennes/`, and the B2B page
  `/amenagement-bureaux-rennes/` grouped under Prestations.
- The cost guide is P0: launch if possible, immediate fast-follow at latest. Phase 3
  should reserve the URL and content-matrix row either way.
- The shop page is a place and brand surface: visitability, scene, thesis line,
  newsletter/Instagram, and artist contact. It is not an art-SEO acquisition branch.
- Do not build an Ille-et-Vilaine page, a renovation service page, "meilleur"
  content, art-buying SEO, or a commune-page network.
- Post-launch order: décoratrice d'intérieur Rennes page after offer validation,
  conseil déco only if the SERP still merits it, a proof-backed Châteaugiron commune
  page, then a renovation guide.
- GBP and local citations deserve launch weight equal to a page: they carry
  Châteaugiron, "meilleur" discovery, and art-place discovery.

## Foundation amendment status

1. **Accepted in foundation v1.2: title pattern.** Section 4 now says keyword-first
   titles apply to acquisition surfaces, while brand-first titles apply everywhere
   else. Acquisition surfaces are the Rennes service page, the cost guide, and the
   B2B page.
2. **Accepted in foundation v1.2: contrat de mission.** Section 7 now says
   architecture work is framed by a clear contrat de mission or lettre de mission
   before paid design work begins.
3. **Deferred, needs Crystelle terms: artist block credibility.** The shop page's
   artist block should eventually address no fee to exhibit, written contract or bon
   de dépôt, clear commission logic, named human contact, and visible place. This
   needs Crystelle's intended commission/dépôt terms.
4. **Deferred post-opening: art price display.** When inventory is real, revisit
   section 9 so the no-numbers pre-opening rule becomes honest visible pricing in
   the shop. No launch amendment is required yet.

No further foundation amendment is needed for audience order, one Rennes service page, public
architecture pricing, Rennes plus Châteaugiron geography, no ecommerce, or no
doorway pages. Phase 1 confirms those foundation rulings.
