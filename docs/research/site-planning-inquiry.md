# Jukkai site-planning inquiry

> Status: working plan, owned by Martin. Not canonical strategy.
> It changes whenever evidence or a conversation reveals a better question.
> Rewritten 2026-07-12 to put Martin's own questions at the spine and collapse
> the calibration/stream scaffolding into one discovery pass.

## Destination

Martin has an evidence-backed and personally understood model for the Jukkai
website: which future `jukkai.fr` pages are needed, what business and discovery
job each performs, what each must communicate and prove, what constraints are
real, and where copy and design remain creatively free. Remaining uncertainty
is explicit.

The concrete output is a selected IA and sitemap plus a short responsibility
brief for every meaningful content page (industry name: a page table), so
copy, design, and build can start without inheriting an agent's hidden
assumptions.

## Martin's questions — the spine

These are Martin's real questions, in his words. Each is tagged by how it gets
answered: **[craft]** = known practitioner knowledge, answered in conversation
and then checked against local evidence; **[evidence]** = needs real Rennes /
old-site / comparison data; **[judgment]** = a Jukkai decision Martin makes
once craft and evidence are in hand.

1. What do I need to surface Jukkai as a top-tier Google result for
   high-value searches in the Rennes area? — _the destination question; it
   decomposes into all the others_
2. What are the competitors doing — what can we extract from them about how
   to rank high? **[evidence]** _(nothing will "prove" why they rank;
   the honest yield is observable differences and competing explanations)_
3. How do you balance editorial versus "SEO-designed" — looking at editorial
   design for architecture, photographers, agencies, maybe real estate:
   local businesses that still show personality? **[craft + evidence]**
4. What is the difference between Rennes Google behaviour and more
   competitive markets? Are there unexplored clusters to capture?
   **[evidence]**
5. How do I know if a cluster/query needs its own page? Why do websites with
   simple sitemaps (home, about, works, contact) rank high? **[craft]**,
   then verified against Rennes data
6. How do I capture décoration, rénovation, and other high-value side
   keywords? **[evidence]**
7. What do I do about the art gallery / shop on the site? **[judgment]**
8. What is the link between GBP and overall website ranking? **[craft]**
9. Backlinks — what material do I need to think about them properly?
   **[craft + evidence]**
10. How doable is it to be relevant for high-value leads outside the Rennes
    area? **[evidence + judgment]**
11. What are professionals looking for — B2B: restaurants, hotels, offices?
    **[evidence + judgment]**
12. Do I need a content strategy — articles, a blog, editorial written
    content separate from the portfolio? **[judgment after evidence]**
13. Why is some sites' hero CTA "discover" while others push instant
    conversion toward contact? **[craft]**
14. Is a per-page strategy best, compared to one comprehensive landing page
    with sections and storytelling as you scroll? **[craft + evidence →
    judgment]**

15. How do I handle the locality of Châteaugiron while ranking high for the
    whole Rennes metro area? Châteaugiron is a small city in the Rennes area,
    but it has charm and the Galerie is a local shop that needs foot traffic —
    while the architecture practice must stay relevant in a large radius
    around Rennes. **[craft + evidence]** _(decomposes into: map-pack reach
    from a Châteaugiron pin — the geo-grid question; organic reach of a
    Rennes-targeted page regardless of pin; and which website surface owns
    which locality. Foundation §8's Rennes/Châteaugiron split is the current
    hypothesis to test.)_

New questions are expected. When one appears, add it here with its tag; do
not answer it merely because an agent noticed it.

## Claims at risk

Confident claims currently floating in the docs, in past agent output, and in
craft conversations. The discovery pass exists to score these: **survives**
(becomes a real constraint), **dies** (becomes creative freedom), or
**unknown** (stays labelled, never masquerades as a requirement).

1. "The new site needs a fixed list of required pages" (previous-method
   agents). Already contradicted by dozens of simple ranking sites; confirm
   locally and bury it.
2. One canonical Rennes service page; the homepage serves the branded
   searcher, the service page serves the stranger (foundation §8 hypothesis).
3. Keyword-first titles are needed only on acquisition surfaces
   (foundation §4/§8).
4. The cost guide is a worthwhile acquisition asset — real "combien coûte…"
   demand exists in the region (foundation §8).
5. Commune pages are only worth shipping with a real project as proof
   (foundation §8).
6. Simple sites rank high in weak markets because relevance + GBP + modest
   authority dominate, not because simplicity works (craft claim).
7. A query earns a dedicated page only with distinct intent, real demand,
   and something distinct to say (craft claim).
8. For "architecte d'intérieur rennes"-type queries, the map pack is worth
   as much as or more than organic position (craft claim).
9. An editorial, story-driven site can still rank locally given a strong GBP
   and basic on-page relevance — counterexamples should exist (Martin's
   observation from references; hunt for them deliberately).
10. National-topic blogging is dead for this business (old data: 4,700
    impressions, 0 clicks — already evidenced, keep unless contradicted).

Falsification caveat: when a counterexample rests on invisible assets (a
ten-year-old domain, accumulated links), score the claim "optional _if_ you
have authority," not "optional."

## The road

Nothing is built, and no page is selected, until the whole evidence base is
gathered and understood. The deliverable of this inquiry is the selected IA,
sitemap, and page briefs — code comes after the destination, not before it.
Gathering is fast (the tooling is live; days, not weeks); understanding is
where the real time goes, and it is not compressed.

1. **Gather — Rennes core** (AFK, in the `/home/martin/src/pro/seo`
   workspace). Owned evidence, the old site including its full audit
   (prescriptions quarantined), and the Rennes field in depth. Scope below.
2. **Corpus check** (short session, Martin + agent). Read the Rennes data
   just enough to fix the query corpus before fanning out — the expedition
   is only comparable if every market gets the same corpus under the same
   parameters. No strategy, no page talk.
3. **Gather — the expedition** (AFK, can be a day or two). The same
   protocol, corpus and parameters held constant, widening ring by ring:
   the department and région (Nantes, Saint-Malo, other Bretagne cities);
   larger French markets (Paris, Lyon, Bordeaux); francophone outside France
   (Montréal); out-of-French big cities (London, Berlin, or as curiosity
   dictates). Plus deep competitor pulls (ranked terms, pages, backlinks)
   for domains the SERPs surfaced.
4. **Curate and study specimens** (Martin-led, parallel to everything).
   The library of well-made local-with-personality sites — photographers,
   agencies, architects, interior designers, real estate, awwwards-ish
   references from anywhere. Tools capture pages; the study is human.
5. **Sensemaking rounds** (sessions, Martin + agent, one per evidence
   block). Read the data together; score the claims at risk; answer the
   spine questions from evidence where evidence applies and from craft
   where it does not. Rounds may send the gathering back out for more.
   Output: disposable notes unless Martin asks otherwise.
6. **Cross-synthesis and decisions.** Only after the material blocks are
   understood: infer the constraint set (apparently necessary / demonstrably
   optional / unknown), settle the judgment questions, prepare anything
   needing Crystelle as an explained recommendation with alternatives.
7. **Whole-site models, then the deliverable.** Compare coherent site
   models against the same accepted evidence; select one; write the sitemap
   and one responsibility brief per meaningful page using the nine
   dimensions in [the method](../operations/method.md). Destination
   reached — copy and design may begin.

### Gathering scope

Every paid call uses explicit France/French parameters (or the target
market's locale in the out-of-French rings) and the intended geography —
never the suite's US/English defaults. Save raw responses with the
provenance manifest from the [capability map](claude-seo-capability-map.md);
keep vendor estimates and suite scores labelled as such.

**Rennes core:**

- **Owned evidence:** confirm the GSC/GA4 properties; one 28-day GSC
  query/page export; one GA4 organic + landing-page summary; PSI/CrUX for the
  current origin (record whether field data exists).
- **Old site:** Firecrawl map of the public site; representative scrapes;
  vendor-ranked terms and backlinks for studioterrasson.fr; the full /seo
  audit may run, with its report saved as agent-authored analysis, never as
  findings.
- **Rennes field:** desktop SERPs at depth 10 across the spine's query
  families (architecte d'intérieur, décoration, rénovation, B2B, Galerie/art);
  batched volume for observed terms; difficulty/intent only if French
  coverage is usable; resolve the business to a stable CID/place ID; public
  GBP profile + review sample; Maps SERPs; a geo-grid across the metro for
  the core keyword (question 15 depends on it — how far a Châteaugiron pin
  reaches).

**Expedition rings:** the same corpus and parameters, only location (and,
in the last ring, language) changing. Store a comparison manifest per ring.

**Competitors:** ranked terms, relevant pages, and backlink samples for
recurring domains — chosen from the evidence, not from reputation.

**Specimens:** full-page captures of each curated site plus its vendor
visibility data, with Martin's human observation notes kept separate from
any tool score.

Skip nothing silently: if a probe returns null, rounded, or empty data,
that is a finding — record it.

## Working rules

- Outputs are for Martin, not for the next agent. Default session contract:
  **nothing written unless Martin asks.** Working notes are disposable.
- Raw data, vendor estimates, and agent recommendations stay visibly
  separate. Recommendations never enter a durable doc without Martin's
  explicit decision.
- `docs/strategy/foundation.md` remains the source for business facts (which
  Martin trusts) and is a claims-at-risk source for its SEO/IA rulings (which
  he does not, yet).
- Crystelle is consulted when a finding would create or materially change a
  business commitment Martin cannot confidently represent.
- The eleven-layer path in [the method](../operations/method.md) stays the
  mental model for what kind of claim is on the table at any moment. It is
  not a schedule.
