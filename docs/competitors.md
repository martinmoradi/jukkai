# Jukkai competitor landscape

**Phase 1 research refresh. Researched live on July 2, 2026.**

Produced per `docs/method.md` (Phase 1, derive mode) using the `competitor-profiling`
skill adapted for a local service business. Reads `docs/foundation.md` v1.1 as frozen
truth; nothing here amends it (a short "implications for foundation" note at the end
confirms consistency). Downstream consumers: Phase 3 (IA/sitemap), Phase 4 (SEO spec,
content matrix).

## Method and limits

- Discovery: live SERPs for Jukkai's likely money searches: "architecte d'intérieur
  Rennes", "architecte d'intérieur Châteaugiron", "tarif / combien coûte architecte
  d'intérieur Rennes", "décoratrice d'intérieur Rennes Ille-et-Vilaine", B2B
  ("aménagement bureaux espaces professionnels Rennes"), and renovation intent.
- Profiling: each shortlisted competitor's live site was fetched page by page
  (homepage, service pages, tarifs, réalisations, à propos, contact) and title/meta
  tags captured from raw HTML. Two sites were down at research date and were profiled
  from Wayback Machine snapshots, flagged inline.
- Limits: searches ran without French geolocation, so the Google local pack and exact
  ranking positions were not measured. "Ranks for X" below means "appeared in organic
  results for X on July 2, 2026". Off-site proof (Google Business Profile reviews,
  Houzz profiles) was not audited; "no reviews visible" means on the website itself.
- Directories (Pages Jaunes, Houzz, Travaux.com, StarOfService, rdvartisans) occupy
  several first-page slots on every money query. They are a SERP feature to coexist
  with, not competitors to profile.

## Landscape overview

The Rennes market for "architecte d'intérieur" intent splits into four camps: small
independent studios with tight single-city SEO (Le Coup de Crayon, Gaëlle Boutaud,
Atelier LŌ, Nolwenn Kevell), a programmatic full-architect incumbent physically in
Châteaugiron (SAAC), national networks running local landing pages (Notes de Styles,
Ynspir), and one haut-de-gamme showroom player (IDKREA) whose site is entirely down
at research date. Nobody combines architecture credibility with an art world. Pricing
transparency is rare but the players who have it (Atelier LŌ, Gaëlle Boutaud, Olivia
Ballet) own the proven cost-intent query cluster. On-site social proof is weak almost
everywhere: no profiled competitor shows a CFAI or UNAID credential, and none embeds
Google reviews.

## Profiles

### 1. Le Coup de Crayon (Laura Jarry)

**URL:** https://www.lecoupdecrayon.com/ · **Seen ranking for:** "architecte
d'intérieur Rennes" (top organic result on our query).

- **Location claim:** Rennes, "Rennes et alentours" in metas. No physical address
  published. No claims beyond the city.
- **Positioning:** solo practitioner brand. "Architecture, décoration d'intérieur &
  mobilier sur-mesure"; specialty claim "les petits espaces qui deviennent de grands
  espaces". A custom-furniture broker arm ("archi-agencement", "un prix attractif en
  éliminant les intermédiaires", circuit court).
- **Audience:** particuliers only. No B2B offer.
- **Services:** two pillars (interior architecture/déco, custom furniture) with
  formalized 4-phase and 6-phase client journeys, ~8-week project presentation with
  full 3D. Paid first appointment, deducted from the devis.
- **Pricing visibility:** none. Pricing method explained (per room, surface,
  prestation) but no figures. No "devis gratuit".
- **Proof:** narrative bio (arts appliqués, école d'architecture intérieure, 8+ years
  in custom furniture, agency founded late 2021). Homepage testimonials only. No
  professional-body credential, no Google reviews widget, no press.
- **Portfolio style:** 12 projects, first-name client naming ("Maison Rennes Ronan et
  Clémence"), locations named, short design narratives on the first few, no budgets,
  surfaces, or systematic before/after. Several marked in progress.
- **CTA:** "Prendre rendez-vous" / "Contactez-moi !", simple form, direct phone and
  email. No booking tool.
- **Local SEO posture:** small clean WordPress site (~7 real pages), every page title
  targets "à Rennes", one keyword-URL service page. Blog dormant (2 posts, one a test
  article). No commune or Ille-et-Vilaine coverage.
- **Title/meta pattern:** `[Service] à Rennes - Le Coup de Crayon` on every page.
- **Strengths:** disciplined per-page Rennes targeting consistent with its top
  ranking; differentiated furniture-broker niche; well-explained process that lowers
  perceived risk; authentic named-client portfolio.
- **Weaknesses:** thin proof layer (no credentials, reviews, or press on site); idle
  content engine; paid first appointment with zero published pricing; solo capacity
  and limited completed-project depth.
- **Art/deco crossover:** none current. One 2022 blog post about rehabilitating a
  Rennes artist's atelier; live sketches offered as a "bonus".

### 2. Alchimie d'intérieur

**URL:** https://www.alchimiedinterieur.com/ · **Seen ranking for:** Rennes,
renovation, and B2B queries (appeared in three of our six searches).

- **Location claim:** based in Andouillé-Neuville (north of Rennes), secondary
  presence in Tinténiac. Claims Rennes, Saint-Malo, Vitré, Dinan, Ille-et-Vilaine,
  "Grand Ouest". The Rennes page is a service-area lander, not a physical presence.
- **Positioning:** generalist duo, harmony/alchemy framing ("projets en Alchimie avec
  vos envies", "à la carte ou clé en main"). Flexible rather than a distinctive
  aesthetic signature.
- **Audience:** particuliers (majority: ~17 of 19 portfolio projects) plus
  professionnels (offices, boutiques, ERP).
- **Services:** broad 9-item offer from déco consultation to full réhabilitation and
  construction support. One named package: "Forfait décoration" (1h30-2h consult,
  written report, optional 3D), price withheld.
- **Pricing visibility:** none anywhere, including the packaged forfait.
- **Proof:** two founders (Steffi Desmas, Adeline Rivalan), "plus de 15 ans" framed
  as duo experience. Decennial insurance stated. 11+ on-site testimonials dated
  2023-2025, Ouest-France article (June 2024), salon presence. No professional-body
  credential visible.
- **Portfolio style:** ~19 codename projects ("Projet Mattone", "Eucalyptus") on one
  long page, locations and surfaces given, before/after photos, narratives, embedded
  testimonials. Strong presentation, but no individual case-study URLs.
- **CTA:** phone-first ("Appelez-nous"), "Se rencontrer" to a form. No booking tool,
  no "devis gratuit" wording.
- **Local SEO posture:** systematic 3 keyword stems x 5 geos = 15 landing pages at
  the root (e.g. `/decorateur-d-interieur-rennes.html`). No Châteaugiron page.
  Actualités nearly dormant (2 posts). Template/agency-built site.
- **Title/meta pattern:** `[keyword] à [ville] - Alchimie d'intérieur`; homepage
  anchors on Andouillé-Neuville, not Rennes.
- **Strengths:** widest local-SERP footprint of the independents via the keyword-city
  matrix; substantial credible portfolio with per-project testimonials; wide offer
  covering B2C and B2B; real offline proof (press, salon).
- **Weaknesses:** zero pricing transparency; no visible credentials and a vague
  experience claim; homepage anchored on a village most prospects don't know;
  templated copy and no content engine.
- **Art/deco crossover:** none. "En magasin" in their déco offer means accompanying
  clients to retail stores, not their own space.

### 3. Atelier LŌ (Lucien Oulhen)

**URL:** https://loatelier.com/ · **Seen ranking for:** "architecte d'intérieur
Rennes" and the tarif cluster (appeared in three of our six searches).

- **Location claim:** 15 Quai Lamennais, Rennes. Claims Rennes plus all four Breton
  departments, names Rennes-metro communes (Cesson-Sévigné, Bruz, Betton, Pacé). No
  Châteaugiron.
- **Positioning:** "Architecte d'intérieur à Rennes & en Bretagne"; single point of
  contact from conception to delivery, photorealistic 3D, technical rigor on complex
  renovations, fee transparency as an explicit differentiator.
- **Audience:** primarily particuliers (22 to 160 m², refresh to heavy renovation and
  new build); homepage mentions professionnels but no distinct B2B offer.
- **Services:** six lines (rénovation, extension, construction neuve, architecture
  d'intérieur, décoration/conseil, 3D). Mission formats: conseil à l'heure, forfait
  conception, mission complète.
- **Pricing visibility:** highest of the full profiles. "À partir de 80€/h" conseil;
  mission complète "8 à 12%" of works budget; an interactive fee simulator with
  concrete bands (relevé 250-550€ by surface; works budgets from 80-180€/m²
  décoratif up to 1 200-2 200€/m² lourd; phased design fees). This plausibly
  explains the tarif-cluster rankings.
- **Proof:** stat blocks ("30+ projets", "7+ années", "100% clients satisfaits"), no
  displayed testimonials, no embedded reviews (the /avis/ page collects reviews
  rather than showing them), no professional-body credential.
- **Portfolio style:** 8 projects with city, type, surface, year. Mostly Finistère
  (Brest, Portsall, Landeda); only one Rennes project despite Rennes-first
  positioning. No budgets, minimal narrative.
- **CTA:** "Demander un devis gratuit", form, phone, email, free first consult, 24h
  response promise, simulator email capture. No Calendly.
- **Local SEO posture:** aggressive. Dedicated `/architecte-interieur-rennes/` page
  with cost and "architecte vs décorateur" FAQ; blog posting every 2-3 days, every
  post Rennes-keyworded at root-level slugs. Content reads templated/AI-cadenced.
- **Title/meta pattern:** keyword-first (`Architecte d'intérieur Rennes | Rénovation
  & Design | Atelier LŌ`), "Devis gratuit" in metas.
- **Strengths:** pricing transparency moat (public rates plus simulator); relentless
  local content cadence; clear technical/3D differentiation; full-spectrum offer.
- **Weaknesses:** zero displayed social proof and an unverifiable satisfaction claim;
  portfolio geography contradicts the Rennes positioning; solo capacity, 7 years;
  thin templated content that may not hold as genuine authority.
- **Art/deco crossover:** none.

### 5. Gaëlle Boutaud

**URL:** https://gaeleboutaud.com/ · **Seen ranking for:** "architecte d'intérieur

- **Location claim:** 10 rue Chicogné, Rennes. Claims Ille-et-Vilaine with communes
  named on the homepage (Cesson-Sévigné, Saint-Grégoire, Bruz, Vitré, Saint-Malo,
  Dinard and others), extends to Côtes d'Armor. Châteaugiron never mentioned.

  ("Transformez votre intérieur... sans erreurs coûteuses"). Two distinctive niches:

  "Étude Complète" (adds coordination and suivi), meublé locatif package, dossiers
  mairie.
- **Pricing visibility:** yes. Book Déco "base forfaitaire de 350 euros" plus "35 à
  50 euros du m²"; Étude Complète 500€ base plus 50-75€/m². The combien-coûte page
  publishes market ranges (conseil 300-800€, études 40-120€/m², accompagnement
  50-250€/m², missions complètes "8% et 15% du montant total des travaux").
- **Proof:** thin. No credentials, no bio or experience claim, minimal testimonials,
  no reviews widget or press. Proof rests on 16 named local projects.
- **Portfolio style:** 16 projects with commune and category, brief problem-solution
  narrative, finished photos only, no budgets/surfaces/before-after, per-project
  targeted CTAs. Administrative dossiers presented as portfolio items.

- **Local SEO posture:** competent single-city depth: keyword-slugged pages

  per-commune pages.
- **Title/meta pattern:** exact-match keyword titles, question format for cost intent
  ("Combien coûte une architecte d'intérieur à Rennes ?").
- **Strengths:** owns the cost conversation with real published figures; two
  defensible query-generating niches (meublé, DP); coherent local content machine;
  low entry price (350€) reduces first-contact friction.
- **Weaknesses:** nearly no credibility layer (no bio, credentials, or reviews);
  underpowered case studies; utilitarian brand with weak aesthetic pull for
  signature-style clients.
- **Art/deco crossover:** none. Shopping lists are a curation deliverable, not
  retail.

## Watchlist (quick scans, not full profiles)

- **Notes de Styles Rennes** (notesdestyles.com, national network): local agency page
  fronted by a named certified architecte d'intérieur/décoratrice; sells forfait
  pricing ("l'une des rares agences à travailler au forfait et pas au pourcentage")
  with national floor prices (conception "à partir de 1 000 €", déco from 300€,

  template prose with thin local soul, beatable on authenticity and design proof.
- **Ynspir Rennes** (ynspir.com): network brand fronting a named local designer;
  region/département/city URL machine across 20+ cities; one pricing line in an FAQ
  (conception "entre 30 et 80 € par mètre carré"); no reviews. Threat: medium; strong
  structure, shallow local content, no Châteaugiron coverage.
- **Olivia Ballet Design** (olivia-ballet.fr): solo independent running the most
  complete independent programmatic play: **52 commune landing pages including
  Châteaugiron**, plus 12 Rennes-intent blog posts led by tariff content. Publishes
  her own rates (visite conseil 120€ TTC, étude 55-75€/m², suivi 10% des travaux).
  Threat: the highest of the watchlist for Châteaugiron-adjacent queries; the
  playbook to match or outflank on cost-intent content.
- **K. Berhault** (k-berhault.fr): the domain currently serves an under-construction
  page with noindex; profiled from a November 2025 snapshot. Had the most complete
  public pricing grid in the market (conseil 70€/h, esquisse 8-14€/m², étude
  40-70€/m² with a 1 500€ minimum, maîtrise d'œuvre 7-9% by bracket, worked
  examples). Threat: near zero today, but the archived grid is the best pricing
  benchmark available, and its disappearance leaves the "transparent grid" slot in
  Rennes momentarily vacant.
- **Also present in SERPs, not profiled:** BōME (Rennes, résidentiel + pro), Krealoft
  (agence + travaux, ~400 réalisations claimed, since 2005), Espaces Intérieurs
  (B2B-leaning cabinet), CMC² Claire-Marie Cousinard (pro spaces, Grand Ouest),
  Spatio (office fit-out + furniture, B2B), and the décoratrice tier (Stéphanie
  Startchenko, Nathalie Bossard UFDI, Solène Montecot, MH Interior). Revisit if
  Phase 4 targets B2B or déco-intent clusters more aggressively.

## Positioning map

Axes chosen for the two decisions that matter to Jukkai: brand register (functional
service vs designed world) and conversion transparency (opaque devis vs public
pricing).

```
                        DESIGNED WORLD / EMOTIONAL REGISTER
                                       |
              IDKREA (showroom,        |     ← JUKKAI TARGET POSITION
              luxury, site down)       |       (art world + public pricing
                                       |        + UNAID proof)

         (sensorial, art discourse)    |
                                       |
   OPAQUE ---------------------------- + ---------------------------- TRANSPARENT
   PRICING                             |                               PRICING

         Le Coup de Crayon             |         Atelier LŌ
         (process story, no prices)    |         (simulator, thin proof)
         SAAC (architect, programmatic)|         Gaëlle Boutaud

         Notes de Styles / Ynspir      |         Olivia Ballet
```

The upper-right quadrant is empty. Every emotionally differentiated player is opaque
on price; every transparent player is functional and proof-thin. That empty quadrant
is exactly where foundation.md already points Jukkai (public architecture pricing,
section 7; Jukkai world as emotional register, section 5).

## Opportunities for Jukkai

1. **Architecture + art is unoccupied.** Nobody in the market sells art, shows
   artists, or has an art place. The closest approximations are IDKREA (curated
   design objects in a showroom, currently offline) and Nolwenn Kevell (art-continuity
   rhetoric with nothing behind it). Jukkai's art shop plus architecture practice is
   a genuinely unclaimed position, provided the site keeps architecture structurally
   dominant per the foundation tiebreak rule. The "poussez la porte" physical-place
   promise also has only one precedent (IDKREA's showroom), and it is dark.
2. **Pricing transparency wins the proven money query, and the bar is low.** The
   tarif/combien-coûte cluster is contested by exactly three players (Atelier LŌ,
   Gaëlle Boutaud, Olivia Ballet), all solo, all proof-thin, and the most radical
   grid (K. Berhault) just went offline. Foundation section 7's public
   ranges/à-partir-de commitment plus a "Combien coûte un architecte d'intérieur à
   Rennes" content page would enter this cluster with a proof layer none of the
   incumbents has. Market reference points for calibration: conseil 70-120€/h,
   études 40-120€/m², missions complètes 7-15% des travaux.
3. **Proof is a market-wide weakness Jukkai can dominate.** No profiled competitor
   shows a CFAI or UNAID credential on site; none embeds Google reviews; several
   show no testimonials at all. Jukkai launches with UNAID, 30 ans de métier (a
   bigger number than any competitor claims: the field claims 7, 15, 15, 20, since
   2006), 2 architectes, carried-over Google reviews, and named projects pending
   selection. Displaying reviews and the credential prominently is table stakes
   nowhere and differentiating everywhere.
4. **Qualified enquiries are nearly unpracticed.** Most funnels are bare contact
   forms or phone-first. Only Nolwenn Kevell qualifies (budget bands to 500K€+,
   start date, file upload), and it coexists with her haut-de-gamme positioning.
   This validates foundation section 3's qualifying form (project type, commune,
   budget band with "je ne sais pas encore"); nobody else will capture
   qualification data plus a booking accelerator.
5. **Châteaugiron is winnable with honest local proof, but not through launch-page
   sprawl.** The commune is contested by SAAC's 19 doorway-style pages (real
   address, real projects, but no interior specialty story, no reviews, dated
   stack), one Olivia Ballet programmatic page, and a Notes de Styles mention. At
   launch, the move is GBP, citations, and physical-anchor copy across the real
   pages. The first dedicated Châteaugiron page belongs post-launch, once it can
   carry a real project. SAAC will keep the full-architect (permits, extensions)
   demand; that segment is not Jukkai's fight.
6. **B2B has room beside one strong incumbent.** Nolwenn Kevell owns institutional
   B2B proof; IDKREA's B2B surface is offline. A Jukkai espaces-professionnels page
   with nameable references (foundation section 6) faces exactly one credible
   independent competitor.
7. **Content quality beats content volume here.** The high-cadence players (Atelier
   LŌ's every-2-3-days blog, SAAC's 1,500 pages) are templated and thin; the
   authentic players barely publish. Foundation section 8's "local content only"
   posture (case studies with location, cost FAQ, a few honest commune pages) is the
   right wedge; it does not need to match anyone's volume.

## Phase 2/3 handoff

- **Messaging:** occupy the empty upper-right quadrant: a designed Jukkai world,
  public architecture pricing, and a proof layer competitors do not combine.
- **Messaging:** do not make "art" carry acquisition. Competitors leave the
  art-world position open, but the competitor and keyword evidence both say the
  searchable demand is architecture, pricing, and B2B.
- **Sitemap:** protect three launch commercial surfaces: the Rennes architecture
  service page, the cost guide, and the espaces-professionnels page. The shop page is
  a brand/place surface, not an SEO acquisition page.
- **Sitemap:** treat Châteaugiron as a launch GBP/citation win and a later honest
  commune page, not as a launch page network. This aligns the competitor findings
  with `keywords.md`.

## Threats to track

- **IDKREA coming back online.** If the site returns (or relaunches rebuilt), the

  before Phase 3 IA freeze and again pre-launch.
- **Network landers escalating.** Notes de Styles already names Châteaugiron; these

- **Atelier LŌ's cadence.** Templated or not, a Rennes-keyworded post every 2-3 days

  Boutaud both publish rates), public pricing alone stops differentiating; the
  pairing with proof is what stays defensible.

## Implications for foundation.md

Nothing found overturns the frozen foundation's business, audience, or positioning
truth. Three confirmations worth recording:

- The competitor-practice claim in foundation section 8 ("matches documented
  competitor practice (Le Rheu, Cesson)") is confirmed live: Nolwenn Kevell operates
  from Le Rheu as "Architecte Intérieur Rennes"; Alchimie claims Rennes from
  Andouillé-Neuville. Claiming Rennes from Châteaugiron is standard practice here,
  and Jukkai does it with a more honest anchor than most.
- The public-pricing decision (section 7) is validated as competitively load-bearing,
  not just honest: it is the entry ticket to the one proven non-branded query
  cluster, and the incumbents holding it are all proof-thin.
- The "30 ans de métier" claim (section 2) outranks every experience claim in the
  profiled field. Worth displaying exactly as canonized.

One non-overturning title-pattern issue from `keywords.md` is now recorded in
foundation v1.2: cost and B2B surfaces may use keyword-first titles because they
compete in acquisition SERPs. This is not a change to the business or audience truth.
