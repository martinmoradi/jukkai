# GBP relocation and rename: cross-check and assessment

**Working research note, produced July 5, 2026.** Trigger: Crystelle saw an
acquaintance's relocated business marked « définitivement fermé » on Google and
asked what Jukkai must do to avoid it. Martin ran the question through a ChatGPT
session (share: chatgpt.com/share/6a4a8444-e64c-83eb-b260-412b3a9ce8ab); this
document cross-checks that session against Google's current documentation and
independent local-SEO sources, records where it agrees or conflicts with the
frozen `docs/strategy/foundation.md` (v1.6), and adds what it missed.

Canon rule applied throughout: where the ChatGPT advice and the foundation
disagree, the foundation wins unless new evidence forces a dated amendment.
Nothing found here requires an amendment. One finding actively strengthens the
foundation's v1.5 GBP ruling (section 4).

## Verdict in three lines

1. The ChatGPT roadmap is fundamentally sound: one profile, evidence before
   edits, never « fermé définitivement », staged migration weeks before launch,
   fallback plan if Google balks. Keep it as the operational skeleton.
2. Its one real error for our case: it recommends the GBP name « Jukkai » or
   « Jukkai Studio ». Foundation v1.5 already ruled the full compound « Jukkai
   by Crystelle Terrasson », and Google's own rebrand criteria make the
   compound the _safer_ option, not just the brand-correct one (section 4).
3. What Crystelle's acquaintance hit is a known, diagnosable, fixable failure
   mode, not evidence that moving kills a business on Google (section 1).

## 1. What probably happened to the acquaintance

Google marks a relocated business « permanently closed » through three known
mechanisms, all documented in Google's own product forums and by local-SEO
practitioners:

1. **The owner (or their agency) created a new profile for the new address**
   and the old profile was closed or flagged. Google explicitly instructs the
   opposite: "Do not create a new Business Profile because of a change in a
   physical location or ownership" (Move your reviews across Business
   Profiles, support.google.com/business/answer/3098204). The new profile
   starts with zero reviews and no history, and the old one shows the red
   label to everyone who searches the name.
2. **A user-suggested edit.** Anyone on Maps can suggest « fermé
   définitivement ». An empty storefront at the old address plus a stale
   citation trail is exactly what prompts a well-meaning Local Guide (or a
   competitor) to file it. If the owner is not monitoring the profile, Google
   applies the edit.
3. **Google's own handling of the old pin.** When an address changes, Google
   sometimes keeps a marker at the old location and labels it permanently
   closed (Sterling Sky: "Google will label your business as permanently
   closed usually when a business has relocated and customers are seeing the
   old location"). The correct state for that old pin is the separate
   **« Moved » status**, which makes the old marker vanish from Search and
   Maps within 24-72 hours instead of displaying the red label. It can be set
   via suggest-an-edit (« déplacé vers un nouvel emplacement ») or by support.

All three are preventable (one-profile discipline, monitoring, fast citation
updates) and the third is fixable after the fact. This is the reassuring,
concrete answer Crystelle's fear deserves: the label is not a fate that
attaches to moving, it is the signature of a migration done without an owner
at the wheel.

## 3. Where the session conflicts with frozen canon

1. **GBP name.** ChatGPT: « Jukkai » or « Jukkai Studio », presented as the
   safe non-stuffed options. Foundation v1.5 (closed decision): « Jukkai by
   Crystelle Terrasson », full compound, because Google weighs the real-world
   identity across website, citations, social bios, and frontage together, and
   because renaming the existing reviewed profile needs « Crystelle
   Terrasson » to survive as the recognizable thread in the map pack where 93%
   of demand is branded. The compound is compliant with the name guidelines: a
   founder's name is a proper noun and part of the consistently represented
   real-world identity, not a service/location keyword or tagline, and the
   v1.5 signage rule (the compound appears somewhere on the physical frontage
   in permanent lettering) is precisely what makes it defensible, including in
   a video verification. **Foundation wins; no change.** ChatGPT simply did
   not know the naming canon.
2. **« Jukkai Studio »** as a candidate public name and **« galerie-
   boutique »** in the suggested GBP description conflict with the naming and
   vocabulary canon (messaging §7/§8: « boutique » is a banned unqualified
   code; the brand nouns are fixed). When the roadmap is folded into our docs,
   its copy examples must be rewritten inside the canon. The transition-post
   idea itself (« Studio Crystelle Terrasson devient Jukkai... ») is good and
   already matches the « anciennement » transition ruling.

## 4. The point ChatGPT missed that works in our favor

Google's rebrand criteria hinge on whether **the proper nouns in the name
survive** the change. « Studio Crystelle Terrasson » → « Jukkai » (ChatGPT's
recommendation) drops the only proper noun, which is exactly the shape Google
calls "a new business". « Studio Crystelle Terrasson » → « Jukkai by Crystelle
Terrasson » keeps the load-bearing proper noun, keeps the primary category
(architecte d'intérieur), and keeps the core service; only the brand word is
new and an activity is added.

So the foundation's v1.5 compound is not just brand strategy and map-pack
continuity: **it is also the strongest possible shape for the rename under
Google's own rebrand eligibility test.** The brand decision and the migration
safety argument point at the same name. Worth stating explicitly to Crystelle,
since it converts her fear into a reason to trust the naming ruling.

## 5. Additions the session did not cover

### 5.1 The « fermé temporairement » cost

Current practitioner testing shows temporarily-closed profiles lose local-pack
visibility and can stop surfacing for discovery queries. Implication for the
move window: if the studio keeps working on client projects through the move
(it does; the shop opens later), **the profile should stay « open » with
honest hours, « sur rendez-vous » if needed**, and temporary closure should be
reserved for a genuine multi-week dead period, kept as short as possible. Do
not park the profile as temporarily closed all summer as a precaution; that
trades a hypothetical risk for a real visibility cost on the branded queries
that feed the pipeline.

### 5.2 Monitoring and defense routine (transition window)

- Weekly check of the profile from a logged-out browser and from Maps: name,
  address, pin position, open status, and any « suggested edit » notifications
  in the GBP dashboard. User edits apply silently when unattended.
- Purge the old address fast everywhere Google reads: website footer and
  contact page, Pages Jaunes, Linktr.ee, social bios, Houzz, directories.
  Stale citations at the old address are what invite « closed » suggestions
  and confuse the entity.
- If a ghost pin appears at the old address labeled « fermé définitivement »:
  do not panic-edit the live profile. Suggest « déplacé vers un nouvel
  emplacement » on the ghost pin (or ask GBP support to set Moved status).
  The marker disappears from Search and Maps within roughly 24-72 hours.
- Keep the phone number stable through the move if at all possible. It is one
  of the strongest continuity signals tying old and new identity together.

### 5.3 Pre-migration review push (already ours, absent from ChatGPT)

The questions file (blocking-for-launch #3) already schedules asking 5-10
recent happy clients for Google reviews on the current profile **before** the
migration; reviews carry over with the profile update. ChatGPT only proposed
fresh reviews after launch. Both are right; the pre-migration push thickens
the profile Google is being asked to trust through the rename.

**Calibration (Martin, July 5, 2026).** A push was already attempted a couple
of months ago with little result; Crystelle is at peak load and the raw
"please ask your clients" framing does not work for her. Two consequences:

- **Stakes stated honestly: nice-to-have, not load-bearing.** The profile
  already carries reviews and they transfer with the rename; the continuity
  evidence Google weighs most is name/signage/website/citation coherence. A
  low-yield push does not endanger the migration, and this must never become
  a guilt item on her list.
- **Reframe as "Martin prepares, Crystelle forwards".** Review asks from busy
  owners fail on friction, not on client goodwill. The kit: the direct
  review short-link, a two-line personal French message, and one ten-minute
  sitting to pick 5-8 recently-delighted clients (delivered in the last year,
  still in contact). She forwards by SMS/WhatsApp, which converts far better
  than email. Her total cost is ten minutes plus pressing send. Best timing
  remains July, before vacations; any project delivering in September gets
  the ask at delivery, the natural high point.

### 5.4 One-session vs staged edits, and the address-before-name question

ChatGPT recommends staging the GBP edits (photos, then address, then website,
then name). Practitioner opinion is split between staging and one clean
coherent edit session. Either is defensible; what is not defensible is
repeated back-and-forth edits over days (looks like hijacking). The playbook
should say: prepare everything, make the edits in one or two sittings in the
recommended order, then freeze and let verification run.

**Sequencing ruling (Martin, July 5, 2026): do not change the address ahead
of the name.** The real timeline (move at the tail end of July, August dead
across the whole industry, public opening October 1) leaves only September as
a « Studio Terrasson at the new address » window, and splitting the edits
across it is the riskier shape, not the safer one:

- An address change forces re-verification, and video verification wants
  permanent signage matching the profile name at the new address. An August
  address change would ask Google to verify « Studio Crystelle Terrasson » at
  a location whose frontage carries Jukkai signage or none yet. Address and
  name together, after the sign is up, means one verification event with one
  coherent story: same business, moved and rebranded.
- Splitting the edits means up to two re-verification events, the first
  running in the month when neither Crystelle nor support turnaround is
  really available.
- The interim mismatch costs nothing. The pipeline is 93% branded validation
  (site, phone, email; none address-dependent), clients only come by
  appointment and are directed personally, the old office is not being
  repurposed so no conflicting claim or « looks closed » storefront signal
  exists, and the Châteaugiron map pack is nearly empty and not a goal before
  October. The old website can carry a moved-to-Châteaugiron line if useful.

So the profile keeps the old address and name until the single migration
window. August gets vacation hours (special hours, never « fermé
temporairement », per 5.1), nothing else.

## 6. Reconciled Jukkai GBP migration playbook (skeleton)

Keyed to existing docs and question keys. Real timeline: office move at the
tail end of July 2026, August is a dead month industry-wide, public opening
October 1, 2026. One GBP migration window, mid-September (5.4).

1. **Now → mid-July: review kit and legal collection.** The Google review
   push (question L3) runs best **now, before the vacations** (August asks go
   unanswered in France), in the low-friction shape from 5.3: Martin prepares
   the link and message, Crystelle picks names in ten minutes and forwards.
   Nice-to-have, not a blocker; a prior push yielded little and that is fine.
   In parallel, collect the lawyer's deliverables as they land: PV, statuts,
   attestation d'annonce légale, Kbis à jour, bail. These double as the GBP
   evidence folder. Touch nothing on Google.
2. **Move window (late July → August).** Physical proof first: permanent
   Jukkai signage with « by Crystelle Terrasson » in small lettering somewhere
   on the frontage (foundation v1.5 signage rule, question L2), storefront and
   interior photos, same phone number. GBP untouched except vacation/special
   hours; no new profile, no permanent closure, no name/address edits, no
   temporary-closed parking (5.1).
3. **Evidence layer (late August → early September).** jukkai.fr live with
   exact NAP, mentions légales, « anciennement Studio Crystelle Terrasson »
   line; old domain 301s with a transition note; socials, Linktr.ee,
   signatures, devis/facture templates updated or staged.
4. **GBP migration (≈ September 10-20, never launch day).** On the existing
   profile, in order: new photos, address (triggers re-verification: be ready
   with signage, premises access, documents), website URL, hours (question
   L5), then name to « Jukkai by Crystelle Terrasson » (foundation v1.5, not
   ChatGPT's bare « Jukkai »). Categories: primary Architecte d'intérieur
   unchanged, add the art-gallery-equivalent secondary. Then stop editing.
   Publish one GBP post announcing the move and the October 1 opening, worded
   inside the vocabulary canon.
5. **Stabilization (late September).** Monitoring routine (5.2). Citations
   sweep (Pages Jaunes, tourism listing, directories) per the Phase 4
   citations checklist. Reply to a few old reviews under the new identity
   where natural.
6. **October 1.** Public communication everywhere; post-opening review asks
   begin.
7. **Fallback if Google refuses the rename or suspends.** Appeal once, with
   the evidence folder, arguing continuity (same company, same founder, same
   primary activity and category, proper noun retained in the name, added art
   activity, new address). Only if Google definitively treats it as a new
   business: create the new profile, verify it, and request the review
   transfer through support. Never reopen a profile Google closed for a
   move/name change; that is the documented dead end.
